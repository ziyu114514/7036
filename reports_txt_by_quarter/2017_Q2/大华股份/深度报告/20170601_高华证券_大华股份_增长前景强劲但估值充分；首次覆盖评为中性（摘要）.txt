投资观点 
我们认为大华将继续在整合之中的安防行业赢得市场份额。截至2016 年，大华
股份是国内第二大、全球第四大视频监控设备及解决方案供应商。我们还预计
随着竞争趋于理性，小企业利润率下降，公司的毛利率将有所改善，且转型后
解决方案业务在收入中的占比将上升。我们预计2016-19 年公司收入年均复合
增速将为32%，盈利年均复合增速将为24%，得益于公司对营销网络和研发实
力的持续投入。然而，鉴于我们的12 个月目标价格为人民币19.00 元，仅意味
着4%的上行空间，我们首次覆盖该股给予中性评级。 
 
主要增长动力 
(1) 海外：未来三年，我们预计海外市场的收入对大华增长的贡献将占三分之
二，在总收入中的占比将从2016 年的38%升至50%。大华已经从一家
OEM/ODM 企业转型为拥有自主品牌的解决方案供应商。(2) 解决方案：在经过
两年从侧重于分销渠道的产品供应商向整体解决方案服务提供商的转型后，
2016 年大华股份的解决方案业务收入在其国内市场占到50%左右的份额，并向
海外市场挺进。我们预计解决方案业务的比重将会继续上升，推动公司盈利能
力改善。 
风险 
政府支出波动，竞争激烈程度改变。 
估值 
我们将行业平均市盈率20 倍乘以大华2018 年预期每股盈利，得出12 个月目标
价格人民币19.0 元。 
行业背景 
安防行业正在经历整合，大企业将从中受益。2016 年下半年行业需求复苏，价
格竞争缓和。

Global branding and sales network expansion 
The surveillance business has a dumbbell-like structure, with sales/marketing network and R&D 
on the two ends. Dahua identifies market share as a very important focus point. As of 2016, we 
estimate Dahua had 8.5% market share in the domestic market and 2.9% market share in the 
overseas market, up from 4.3% and 0.5% in 2011. Currently, overseas revenue accounts for 38% 
of the company total, and management expects to increase this to 60%-70% over the next three 
to five years. We expect overseas revenue to reach 50% of total revenue in 2019E. 
In terms of sales network, Dahua has over 2,000 in-house sales staff for the domestic markets, 
covering 280 regions and 3,000 counties in China. Its distribution networks reach further into the 
countryside. Dahua classifies its customers into three groups: 1) smart city/safe city; 2) big 
enterprise; and 3) SME/retail customers. For city-level projects, Dahua would send in an entire 
team to be based in the city, communicating with clients on a daily basis. For big enterprise 
customers, Dahua uses in-house sales to cover on a consistent basis. And for SME/retail 
customers, the distribution channel is put in play. 
For the overseas market, Dahua is transforming itself away from being an OEM to overseas 
surveillance product/service providers by: 1) promoting its own-branded products through 
distributors; and 2) winning solutions project orders from country/city governments (for example, a 
border project in Hungary, a safe city project in Mexico, a police force project in Turkey). There’re 
over 1,000 in-house salespeople covering overseas markets for Dahua, 100 local salespeople in 
North America facing big system integrators. So far Dahua has set up 35 subsidiaries outside 
China, and management identifies global expansion to be a very important strategy. Management 
is planning to further invest in and expand its global sales network in the future.

Focus on R&D  
Dahua positions itself as a technology company, and it is continuously focused on R&D. As of 
end 2016, Dahua had 5,321 employees in its R&D department (61% of total), and R&D expense 
as a percentage of revenue has consistently increased from 5.8% in 2007 to 10.7% in 2017. 
Going forward, management expects R&D expense as a percentage of revenue to stay above 
10% at least. Dahua established four research institutes in 2015-2016, focusing on chip, big data, 
artificial intelligence, and advanced applications. The surveillance front-end industry has lived 
through two waves of technology upgrade previously: (1) from analog camera to IP camera; and 
(2) from isolated network structure to connected network structure. Looking forward, we believe 
we are at the initial stage of the next wave of upgrade to AI-embedded surveillance products and 
systems. We are already seeing the change in the industry; surveillance is evolving from simply 
being the eyes of public and private sectors to being the eyes and brains in that it not only 
captures videos and images, but also does more and more complex analysis to help with various 
business needs. In this new intelligent era, Dahua strives to be the intelligent IoT solutions 
provider and service operator with a focus on video.

Financials 
Income statement 
 
In 2005-2016, Dahua grew its revenue by 45X from Rmb293mn to Rmb13,329mn, at a 
CAGR of 41%, mainly driven by the growth in its front-end product suite, the share of which 
increased from 1% of overall revenue in 2006 to 49% in 2016. This is mainly driven by the 
rising penetration of surveillance cameras in China, as well as overseas markets. By 2020E, 
we expect Dahua’s revenue to reach Rmb38,121mn, at a CAGR of 30% from 2016. Front-
end products will still be a big driver of this growth with incremental as well as 
upgrade/replacement demand for cameras, and its contribution will increase to 57% by 
2020E, based on our estimates. 
 
In 2005-2016, Dahua grew its EPS by 30X from Rmb0.02 to Rmb0.63, or a CAGR of 36%. 
We notice that EPS growth lagged Dahua’s revenue growth, mainly as a result of declining 
gross margins due to competition. By 2020E, we expect Dahua’s EPS to reach Rmb1.57, up 
by a CAGR of 26% from 2016, slower than the revenue CAGR as a result of rising sales and 
marketing spend on overseas expansion. The surveillance industry has been consolidating 
with big players such as Dahua gaining market share on a consistent basis. In 2016, gross 
margin improved by 0.5ppt after two years of decline, and net margin improved by 0.1ppt 
after two years of decline. On the other hand, we note small players’ profitability level 
continues to deteriorate, with some listed companies turning negative in 2016. As a result, 
we believe Dahua’s gross margin should continue to improve going forward.

Introducing M&A framework for Dahua 
We have previously examined our Asia Pacific technology coverage using an M&A framework 
(see Navigating the noise (6): Introduce M&A framework, update roadmap, July 4, 2016). In the 
framework, we examine companies’ potential as M&A targets and rank them from 1 to 4 based on 
our assessment of three key factors.  
Firstly, we look at whether there are any impediments, such as shareholder 
structure/preferences, industry regulations/government policies, and takeover defense 
strategies that could prevent a transaction from occurring. We then consider four qualitative 
factors 1) attractiveness of technology; 2) attractiveness of customers; 3) cost synergy potential; 
and 4) other/intangible value, and two quantitative factors: 1) market cap; and 2) valuations. 
For companies ranked 1 or 2, we incorporate an M&A component into our target price. 
Shareholder structure and potential impediments 
We gauge whether there are any impediments to M&A because, if there are, a company is 
unlikely to become an M&A target even if it ranks 1 or 2 on qualitative/quantitative criteria. 
When assessing potential impediments, we take into consideration:  
1) 
major shareholder preferences (we assume that shareholders with a 20% or greater 
stake have significant influence, or a blocking stake), and take into account: any 
potential actions by major shareholders in the event of a possible acquisition by a third 
party (including whether they would likely be receptive, or if they may be opposed to 
selling), or if they have announced a preference to raise their stake in the company;  
2) 
regulatory and or government policy impediments; and  
3) 
possible takeover defense measures such as a poison pill. 
We assign a “No” qualification if one or more of the three considerations above are applicable and 
a “Yes” qualification if these considerations do not apply. 
Four qualitative assessment criteria 
1) 
Technology: We look at whether or not a company has attractive technology capable of 
generating future value, as well as its technological capabilities and intellectual property 
rights. We assign a rank of “4” if its technology is unattractive, “3” if it is moderately 
attractive, “2” if it is attractive and “1” if it is very attractive. 
2) 
Customer base: We look at whether or not a company has an attractive customer base 
and product applications. We also take customer retention and end-market exposure 
(including sales channels) into account. We assign a rank of “4” if its customer base and 
product applications are unattractive, “3” if they are moderately attractive, “2” if they are 
attractive and “1” if they are very attractive. 
3) 
Cost synergies: We look at prospects for growth in business scale and scope for profit 
margin/ yield improvement via acquisition of an M&A target company. We assign a rank 
of “4” if there is low possibility of synergies, “3” if there is some possibility , “2” if there is 
high possibility, and “1” if there is a very high possibility. 
4) 
Other: We look at whether or not a target company possesses attractive assets, 
including brand strength, outstanding management/employees, and intangible assets. 
We assign a rank of “4” if they are unattractive, “3” if they are moderately attractive, “2” if 
they are attractive, and “1” if they are very attractive.

1) 
Market cap: We rank the companies in four tiers, as shown below, based on the

Key risks 
1. 
Fluctuation in government/public spending 
As government- and public-related projects accounted for over 40% of total domestic 
demand in 2016, we see fluctuation risks if there are any policy changes at the central 
government level and spending delays at the local government level.  
For example, in 2014-2015, demand in government-related projects slowed due to slowing 
macroeconomic growth in China. This led to Hikvision’s revenue growth slowing down to 
36% yoy and 37% yoy in 2014 and 2015, from 53% yoy in 2013. Starting in 2H16, industry 
participants have noted a recovery in demand, and this could pose an upside risk to our 
forecasts. 
2. 
Competitive intensity 
There are over 1,500 surveillance product providers in China. Although Dahua’s size is much 
larger than the rest, it is still not insulated from pricing competition, especially in the relatively 
low-end, standardized distribution channels.  
ASP for mature surveillance products generally declines by 6%-7% yoy. However, in periods 
of weaker demand, surveillance product providers tend to cut pricing more aggressively in 
order to protect market share and maintain revenue growth. On the other hand, products with 
technology leadership and limited supply tend to have a high pricing premium initially.

投资摘要 
投资摘要部分通过将一只股票的主要指标与其行业和市场相比较来评价该股的投资环境。所描述的四个主要指标包括增长、回报、估值倍数和波动性。增长、
回报和估值倍数都是运用数种方法综合计算而成，以确定该股在地区研究行业内所处的百分位排名。  
每项指标的准确计算方式可能随着财务年度、行业和所属地区的不同而有所变化，但标准方法如下：  
增长是下一年预测与当前年度预测的综合比较，如每股盈利、EBITDA 和收入等。 回报是各项资本回报指标一年预测的加总，如CROCI、平均运用资本回报率
和净资产回报率。 估值倍数根据一年预期估值比率综合计算，如市盈率、股息收益率、EV/FCF、EV/EBITDA、EV/DACF、市净率。 波动性根据12 个月的历史
波动性计算并经股息调整。  
Quantum 
Quantum 是提供具体财务报表数据历史、预测和比率的高盛专有数据库，它可以用于对单一公司的深入分析，或在不同行业和市场的公司之间进行比较。

买入、中性、卖出：分析师建议将评为买入或卖出的股票纳入地区投资名单。一只股票在投资名单中评为买入或卖出由其相对于所属研究行业的总体潜在回报决

委员会根据25-35%的股票评级为买入、10-15%的股票评级为卖出的全球指导原则来管理该地区的投资名单；但是，在某一特定分析师所覆盖行业中买入和卖
