人工智能业务前景明朗，但估值已合理；首次覆盖评为中性 (摘要) 
投资观点 
我们认为，全球最大的监控产品供应商——海康威视在人工智能产品领域较同
业拥有显著的竞争优势。海康威视可从其规模庞大的客户群当中获取大量数据
集，这对于训练深度学习算法至关重要。此外，海康威视的规模优势和雄厚的
国企背景有望为稳定的毛利率提供支撑。但今年以来该股股价已上涨了62%，
当前市盈率位于19 倍的12 个月历史均值附近，而且与全球同业相符。我们首
次覆盖该股评为中性，因为我们认为其当前估值已充分体现了公司坚实的基本
面。 
 
主要增长动力 
我们预计，到2020 年海康威视的人工智能产品对总收入和总毛利的贡献将分别
达到12%和17%（2016 年为0.3%和0.5%）。除人工智能外，我们认为公司
凭借其解决方案业务和高质量产品的规模优势和专业实力将继续实现市场份额
增长。鉴于2016 年下半年以来行业竞争趋于理性、管理层侧重于提升盈利能
力，我们预计2017-2019 年毛利率将稳定在42%左右（2016 年为41.6%）。 
风险 
政府/公共支出波动，竞争激烈程度发生变化。 
估值 
我们预计2016-2019 年收入/盈利年均复合增速为29%，受到市场份额持续扩张
和盈利水平企稳的推动。我们对2018 年预期每股盈利应用20 倍的行业平均市
盈率，计算得出12 个月目标价格人民币41.0 元，隐含5%的上行空间。 
行业背景 
监控行业正在经历的整合对大企业有利。2016 年下半年行业需求复苏，价格竞
争缓解。

Executive Summary: Strong fundamentals priced in; Neutral 
Hikvision has grown and consolidated its position as a global surveillance industry leader over the 
past decade, with its focus on R&D, growing scale advantage, and leveraging its SOE 
background effectively to win projects from public sector clients. We believe this has also been 
underpinned by the last two waves of technology upgrades from: (1) analog camera to IP camera, 
and (2) isolated network structure to connected network structure.  
 
Looking forward, we expect the next wave of upgrades to AI-embedded surveillance products 
and systems to further expand Hikvision’s market share and raise/stabilize its gross margin. 
Hikvision has been the leader in introducing AI surveillance products into the market since 
2015, from cameras to network video recorder (NVR) to servers. For example, its products 
have been adopted by Nanchang City’s SkyNet project, effectively improving the accuracy 
and speed of identifying potential suspects and security breaches.  
 
In the AI era, we believe Hikvision has a strong competitive advantage with significant access 
to relevant data sets from its large customer base. Our global AI report (Artificial Intelligence: 
AI, Machine Learning and Data Fuel the Future of Productivity, dated Nov 14, 2016) identifies 
four key inputs for profit pool creation as we move towards an AI-centric world: data, talent, 
infrastructure and silicon; and ranks large differentiated data sets as the most likely driver of 
growth.  
Valuations fair, initiate with Neutral 
We initiate on Hikvision with Neutral and a 12-month target price of RMB41.0, implying 5% 
potential upside. We apply 20X industry average P/E multiple to Hikvision’s 2018E EPS to arrive 
at our target price. 20X is the average P/E multiple of Hikvision’s global and domestic peers in 
2018E. All the stocks included have above US$1bn market cap.  
We rate Hikvision Neutral on valuation grounds. Post a 62% rally in its stock price ytd, the 
stock is now trading around its 12-month historical average P/E multiple of 19X, and in line with 
its global peers. Key risks to our view and target price include fluctuations in government/public 
spending and changing competitive intensity. 
Competitive edge: Scale advantage and SOE background 
Being the largest surveillance provider in the world, Hikvision has scale advantage in terms of 
its: (1) sales force and distribution network, (2) R&D team and spending, (3) economies of scale 
in SG&A, (4) pricing power with its suppliers, and (5) data sets. These scale advantages reflect in 
its above-average gross margin and industry leading net margin among Chinese surveillance 
companies. As we move towards an AI-centric world, scale advantage has gained an incremental 
aspect of interpretation – data – which is critical for training deep learning algorithms, in our view. 
Leveraging its SOE background and relationship with the public sector (i.e. public security, 
transportation, judicial, etc.), we believe Hikvision is in a strong position to provide surveillance 
solutions to these customers. Public security (i.e., police force) and transportation segments have 
the strongest incentive to adopt state-of-the-art surveillance solutions, in our view. 
Earnings growth to catch up with revenue growth by 2020E 
 
We expect Hikvision’s revenue to reach RMB83,231mn in 2020E, at a CAGR of 27% from 
2016. Front-end products will still be a big driver for this growth with incremental as well as 
upgrade/replacement demand for cameras. On the other hand, we see control center 
equipment’s share of revenue to increase from 12% in 2016 to 29% in 2020E as we believe,

Key risks 
1. 
Fluctuation in government/public spending 
As government and public-related projects accounted for over 40% of total domestic demand 
in 2016, we see fluctuation risks if there are any policy changes at the central government 
level and spending delays at local government level.  
For example, during 2015-2016, demand in government related projects slowed due to 
slowing macro economic growth in China. This led to Hikvision’s revenue growth slowing 
down to 47% yoy and 26% yoy in 2015 and 2016, from 60% yoy in 2014. Starting 2H16, 
industry participants have noted a recovery in demand, and this could pose an upside risk to 
our forecasts. 
2. 
Competitive intensity 
There are over 1,500 surveillance product providers in China. Although Hikvision’s size is 
much larger than the rest, this still not insulate it from pricing competition, especially in the 
relatively low-end, standardized distribution channels.  
ASP for mature surveillance products generally declines at a rate of 6%-7% yoy. However, in 
periods of weaker demand, surveillance product providers tend to cut pricing more 
aggressively in order to protect market share and maintain revenue growth. On the other 
hand, products with technology leadership and limited supply tend to have a high pricing 
premium initially. 
Scale and SOE background set Hikvision apart 
Over the past 16 years, Hikvision has established a strong competitive advantage that sets itself 
part from competitors in both the domestic as well as overseas markets. In China, Hikvision was 
initially able to leverage its SOE background and public sector relationships to supply into high-
demand sectors such as police and transportation. Then with its product/service experience and 
expertise, it expanded into enterprise sectors such as financials, property, and energy. In overseas 
markets, it started out from the distribution channels with high-quality products and competitive pricing, 
and later entered into solutions service.  
16 years later, it has grown to the No.1 surveillance company globally. Going forward, we believe 
Hikvision could leverage its scale advantage to provide advanced high-quality products and 
services and continue to gain market share worldwide. As we advance into the AI era, scale 
advantage has gained an incremental aspect of interpretation – data – which is paramount for 
training deep learning algorithms.  
Our global AI report (Artificial Intelligence: AI, Machine Learning and Data Fuel the Future of 
Productivity, dated Nov 14, 2016) identifies four key inputs for profit pool creation as we move 
towards an AI-centric world: data, talent, infrastructure and silicon; and ranks “large 
differentiated data sets” as the most likely driver of growth. Hikvision’s large customer base 
gives it a headstart vs peers on the AI front, in our view. 
Scale provides competitive advantage 
Being the largest surveillance provider in the world, Hikvision has scale advantage in terms of its 
sales force and distribution network, R&D team and spending, economies of scale in SG&A, and 
pricing power with its suppliers. 
Hikvision is the world’s largest video surveillance provider, with about 10% global market share in 
2016. It has 35 offices/branches throughout China, and 28 offices around the world. Leveraging

comprehensive product portfolio from front-end and back-end to control center

5. Data sets – critical for AI 
Our global AI report (Artificial Intelligence: AI, Machine Learning and Data Fuel the Future of 
Productivity, dated Nov 14, 2016) identifies four key inputs for profit pool creation as we move 
towards an AI-centric world: data, talent, infrastructure and silicon; and ranks “large 
differentiated data sets” as the most likely driver of growth. Being the largest company in the 
surveillance industry, Hikvision has an industry leading customer base across seven 
broad sectors: public security, transportation, civil, judicial, financial, energy, and building. With 
its broad customer base comes large amounts of data sets — these highly relevant data sets 
could be used in training Hikvision’s deep learning algorithms with high performance computers 
using Nvidia GPUs. We note that the more data a training algorithm “learns”, the more intelligent it 
will become when conducting analysis with live data sets. As Andrew Ng – founder of Coursera 
and former Chief Scientist at Baidu – puts it: “Deep learning is (almost) always about scale.” 
SOE background  
Hikvision was established in 2001 by the No.52 Research Institute of China Electronics 
Technology Group Corporation (CETC), which is under the State-owned Assets Supervision and 
Administration Commission (SASAC). CETC is mainly involved in the research, development, and 
production of large-scale electronic information systems, communication and electronic 
equipment, software and key components for major national military and civilian applications.  
Leveraging its SOE background and relationship with the public sector (i.e. public security, 
transportation, judicial, etc.), Hikvision is in a strong position to provide surveillance solutions to 
these customers, in our view. We believe the public security (i.e. police force) and transportation 
segments have the strongest incentive to adopt state-of-the-art surveillance solutions as the 
outcome could be immediate, i.e. helping the police department catch alleged suspects more 
efficiently and effectively, and helping the transportation department identify vehicles breaking the 
law and issue fines. As a result, these government-related projects focus more on technology and 
delivery rather than pricing, hence we see weaker pricing competition in the solutions business.

Front-end products

leading the industry in terms of capturing clearer, cleaner and brighter color images in foggy, 
starlight and darker environments. Going forward, we expect front-end products to continue to be 
a big revenue contributor and grow at a CAGR of 18% over 2016-2019E.

Financials 
Income statement 
 
From 2008 to 2016, Hikvision grew its revenue by 18X from RMB1,742mn to RMB31,924mn, 
at a CAGR of 44%, mainly driven by the growth in its front-end product suite, the share of 
which increased from 7% of overall revenue in 2008 to 50% in 2016. This is mainly driven by 
the rising penetration of surveillance cameras in China, as well as overseas markets. By 
2020E, we expect Hikvision’s revenue to reach RMB83.231mn, at a CAGR of 27% form 2016. 
Front-end products will still be a big driver for this growth with incremental as well as 
upgrade/replacement demand for cameras. On the other hand, we see control center 
equipment’s share of revenue to increase from 12% in 2016 to 29% in 2020E as we believe, 
in the AI era, with growing focus on big data and intelligent analysis of these data sets, the 
bulk of the computing will be done by servers in the control/data centers. 
 
From 2008 to 2016, Hikvision grew its EPS by 12X from RMB0.10 to RMB1.22, or a CAGR of 
36%. We notice that EPS growth lagged Hikvision’s revenue growth, mainly as a result of 
declining gross margins, from 51% to 42%. The gross margin pressure was from: (1) market 
competition, and (2) distribution channel business. By 2020E, we expect Hikvision’s EPS to 
reach RMB3.14, at a CAGR of 29%, same as the revenue CAGR. The surveillance industry 
has been going through consolidation with big players such as Hikvision gaining market 
share on a consistent basis. In 2016, gross margin improved for the first time over the past 
seven years for Hikvision, and net margin stabilized after three years of decline. On the other 
hand, we note small players’ profitability level continuing to deteriorate, with some listed 
companies turning negative in 2016. As a result, we think Hikvision’s gross margin should 
stabilize/improve going forward, leading to earnings growth catching up with revenue growth. 
Balance sheet 
 
Hikvision has consistently operated at a net cash position over the past decade. As of 2016, 
its net cash to EBITDA ratio was 1.24X, implying a strong balance sheet.  
 
ROE was 34% in 2016, relatively stable since 2010, and we expect it to increase to 40% in 
2019E with improving asset turn. 
 
Cash conversion cycle also improved to 58 days in 2016 from 94 days in 2010, and we 
expect this to stabilize over the next three years.

ratio from 2017E to 2019E.

Introducing M&A framework for Hikvision 
In our report Navigating the noise (6): Introduce M&A framework, update roadmap, dated July 4, 
2016), we examined stocks in our Asia Pacific technology coverage using our M&A framework — 
considering impediments that could prevent a transaction from occurring as well as qualitative 
and quantitative factors — to incorporate the potential that certain companies could be acquired 
at a premium to current share prices.  
We then assign a M&A score as a means of ranking companies under coverage from 1 to 4, with 
1 representing high (30%-50%) probability of M&A activity, 2 representing medium (15%-30%) 
probability, 3 representing low (10%-15%) probability and 4 representing minimal to no probability 
(0%-10%). For companies ranked 1 or 2, in line with our standard departmental guidelines we 
incorporate an M&A component into our target price. 
Firstly, we look at whether there are any impediments, such as shareholder 
structure/preferences, industry regulations/government policies, and takeover defense that 
could prevent a transaction from occurring. We then consider four qualitative factors, including: 
1) attractiveness of technology, 2) attractiveness of customers, 3) cost synergy potential, 4) 
other/intangible value; and two quantitative factors, 1) market cap, and 2) valuations. 
Shareholder structure and potential impediments 
We gauge whether there are any impediments to M&A, because if there are, a company is 
unlikely to become an M&A target even if it ranks 1 or 2 on qualitative/quantitative criteria. 
When assessing potential impediments we take into consideration:  
(1) major shareholder preferences (we assume that shareholders with a 20% or greater stake 
have significant influence, or a blocking stake), and take into account: any potential actions by 
major shareholders in the event of a possible acquisition by a third party (including whether they 
would likely be receptive, or if they may be opposed to selling), or if they have announced a 
preference to raise their stake in the company;  
(2) regulatory and or government policy impediments; and  
(3) possible takeover defense measures such as a poison pill. 
We assign a “No” qualification if one or more of the three considerations above are applicable and 
a “Yes” qualification if these considerations do not apply. 
 
Four qualitative assessment criteria 
1) Technology: We look at whether or not a company has attractive technology capable of 
generating future value, as well as its technological capabilities and intellectual property rights. 
We assign a rank of “4” if its technology is unattractive, “3” if it is moderately attractive, “2” if it is 
attractive and “1” if it is very attractive. 
2) Customer base: We look at whether or not a company has an attractive customer base and 
product applications. We also take customer retention and end-market exposure (including sales 
channels) into account. We assign a rank of “4” if its customer base and product applications are 
unattractive, “3” if they are moderately attractive, “2” if they are attractive and “1” if they are very 
attractive. 
3) Cost synergies: We look at prospects for growth in business scale and scope for profit 
margin/ yield improvement via acquisition of an M&A target company. We assign a rank of “4” if 
there is low possibility of synergies, “3” if there is some possibility , “2” if there is high possibility, 
and “1” if there is very high possibility.

4) Other: We look at whether or not a target company possesses attractive assets, including

Two quantitative assessment criteria 
1) Market cap: We rank the companies in four tiers based on the assumption that the smaller a 
company is, the easier it is to acquire. We also take regional qualities (such as differing market 
cap ranges between regions) into account. 
2) Valuation: We look at whether a target company’s three-year average P/E and EV/DACF are 
below the industry/peer average for the same period, as we believe companies trading at a 
discount to peers are more likely to be acquired.  
We assign a rank of “4” if the company appears overvalued relative to its respective industry peer 
group, “3” if its valuation is in line with the peer average, “2” if its valuation is discounted, and “1” if 
its valuation is significantly discounted. We take into account differing valuation multiple ranges 
between the companies depending on their region and their peers. 
Within this framework, we assign an M&A rank of ‘3’ to Hikvision. 
This is mainly due to its relatively large market cap of US$33,983mn, SOE background and 
shareholding structure (40% held by the Zhejiang Haikang Group whose shareholding has been 
stable over time). We believe the company is more likely to be an acquirer rather than a target.

投资摘要 
投资摘要部分通过将一只股票的主要指标与其行业和市场相比较来评价该股的投资环境。所描述的四个主要指标包括增长、回报、估值倍数和波动性。增长、
回报和估值倍数都是运用数种方法综合计算而成，以确定该股在地区研究行业内所处的百分位排名。  
每项指标的准确计算方式可能随着财务年度、行业和所属地区的不同而有所变化，但标准方法如下：  
增长是下一年预测与当前年度预测的综合比较，如每股盈利、EBITDA 和收入等。 回报是各项资本回报指标一年预测的加总，如CROCI、平均运用资本回报率
和净资产回报率。 估值倍数根据一年预期估值比率综合计算，如市盈率、股息收益率、EV/FCF、EV/EBITDA、EV/DACF、市净率。 波动性根据12 个月的历史
波动性计算并经股息调整。  
Quantum 
Quantum 是提供具体财务报表数据历史、预测和比率的高盛专有数据库，它可以用于对单一公司的深入分析，或在不同行业和市场的公司之间进行比较。

买入、中性、卖出：分析师建议将评为买入或卖出的股票纳入地区投资名单。一只股票在投资名单中评为买入或卖出由其相对于所属研究行业的总体潜在回报决
定。任何未获得买入或卖出评级且拥有活跃评级（即不属于暂停评级、暂无评级、暂停研究或没有研究的股票）的股票均被视为中性评级。每个地区投资评估
委员会根据25-35%的股票评级为买入、10-15%的股票评级为卖出的全球指导原则来管理该地区的投资名单；但是，在某一特定分析师所覆盖行业中买入和卖
出评级的分布可能根据地区投资评估委员会的决定而有所不同。此外，每个地区投资评估委员会管理着地区强力买入或卖出名单，该名单以总体潜在回报规模
和/或实现回报的可能性为主要依据确立各自研究范围内的投资建议。将股票加入或移出此类强力买入或卖出名单并不意味着分析师对这些股票的投资评级发生
了改变。 
总体潜在回报：代表当前股价低于或高于一定时间范围内预测目标价格的幅度，包括所有已付或预期股息。分析师被要求对研究范围内的所有股票给出目标价
格。总体潜在回报、目标价格及相关时间范围在每份加入投资名单或重申维持在投资名单的研究报告中都有注明。
