Heading for growth spurt from first Gen6 
flexible AMOLED line 
BOE unveiled China’s first, world’s second Gen6 flexible AMOLED 
display production line. BOE Technology, a leading display manufacturer, 
has pulled the trigger on its Chengdu-based Gen6 flexible active-matrix 
organic light-emitting diode (AMOLED) line–built in the space of less 
than five months, well ahead of schedule. At a delivery ceremony, the 
company handed the first batch of its Gen6 flexible AMOLED panels to 
top Chinese handset makers such as Huawei, Oppo, Vivo, Xiaomi, ZTE and 
nubia. The new launch would significantly boost BOE’s standing in the 
flexible OLED field. 
Rising flexible OLED display demands spur and sustain future growth. 
Some of the world’s prominent smartphone players are jockeying to play 
the ace of flexible OLED for their “next big thing”, as witnessed by Apple’s 
patented iPhone design featuring a bifold or trifold screen thought to hit 
the market sometime, and Samsung’s debuting this year of a futuristic 
OLED panel able to be stretched, scrolled and folded. This, coupled with 
growing flexible OLED demands emanating from other segments like 
wearables, VR devices, tablets and notebooks, would benefit an industry 
leader like BOE, which is now in a better position for a potential deluge 
of bendable and foldable display orders mostly from well-famed mobile 
devices and wearables producers. 
BOE’s profitability set to shoot up as flexible OLED line yield improves. 
A single 48,000-unit flexible OLED line is expected to close in on the 
breakeven point on a 50% yield, which, when ramps up to 80%, would 
lead to an annual profit of about RMB14bn–outstripping BOE’s LCD 
arm’s 2017 profit–and a ROE of nearly 30%, well above roughly 10% for 
the company as a whole for the current full year. Hence, BOE looks set to 
significantly shore up its profitability through yield and capacity increase 
at its flexible OLED business, in our view.

1. BOE unveiled China’s first, world’s second Gen6 flexible AMOLED 
display production line 
BOE Technology Group Co., Ltd., a leading display maker, announced on Oct. 26 it had kick-started mass production from 
its first Gen6 flexible AMOLED display production line, which is based in Chengdu. At its launch event, the company 
delivered the first batch of its Gen6 flexible AMOLED panels to high-profile customers such as Huawei, Oppo, Vivo, 
Xiaomi, ZTE and nubia.

2. BOE’s profitability set to surge as flexible OLED line yield improves 
BOE’s first flexible OLED line was built in the space of less than five months, with its yield ramp-up time much shorter 
than that of Samsung in its early stage. This, we believe, can be credited to the company’s technological accumulation 
in OLED as well as its adoption of first-class equipment and materials. BOE boasts to have engaged in OLED R&D for eight 
years (five years on flexible) and obtained 55,000 patents (16,000 concerning OLED).

assuming a unit cost of USD80, and an 80% yield to generate an annual revenue/profit of around RMB75bn/13.85bn 
and an ROE of nearly 30% (well above its current full-year level of about 10% for the company as a whole) assuming a 
unit cost of USD65, and it is estimated that at its full capacity, a single such production line will create a profitability 
surpassing the company’s LCD division’s full-year profitability in 2017. Hence, improved yield and capacity at BOE’s 
flexible OLED business will dramatically drive up its overall profitability and provide an impetus for medium- and long-
term business growth, in our view.

In light of BOE’s announced figures regarding its two flexible OLED display production lines’ capacity and yield 
improvement, as well as related market price changes, our neutral estimate is that the company’s flexible OLED business 
will bring in an operating revenue of RMB2.649bn/26.608bn/80.915bn for FY17-19, a step change going forward.

Our neutral projection on the combined revenue from BOE’s nine existing LCD production lines stands at 
RMB92.997bn/106.955bn/110.729bn for FY17-19. In our view, the LCD market is approaching a supply-demand 
equilibrium, with possibility of seasonal price fluctuations but low visibility of deep price corrections due to a 
concentration on capacity release seen in the past. Hence, the company’s LCD profitability looks set to hold steady, in 
our view. 
BOE currently has a relatively small percentage of production lines below Gen6, which have mostly been reoriented 
towards higher margin areas such as white goods, IVI and RFID, thus having a limited impact on the company’s overall 
profitability performance. Moreover, the company boasts to be the only in the world whose Gen8.5 production line can 
perform mobile phone panel cutting, and such high-gen lines also have a cost advantage in small- and medium-sized 
products, thereby further contributing to the company’s profitability and competitiveness.

3. Rising demands for flexible OLED displays spur and sustain future 
growth 
Assuming a capacity utilization rate of 90% and a yield of 85%/65% for rigid/flexible screens, we estimate global supply 
of 5.5-inch OLED mobile phone screens will climb from 470mn (180mn/290mn for flexible/rigid) in 2017 to 790mn 
(420mn/370mn for flexible/rigid) in 2018 and 1.26bn (720mn/540mn for flexible/rigid) in 2019, while global demands 
for such products will rise from 490mn in 2017 to 770mn/1.1bn/1.39bn/1.71bn in 2018/2019/2020/2021. Hence, a 
supply-demand equilibrium is expected to arrive around 2019, but we believe global OLED display production capacity 
expansion will charge on nonetheless, on the back of a 50% penetration for mobile phones, growing market potential 
for foldable screens, and relatively low levels of take-up for such products as VR devices, tablets and notebooks.  
Supply side  
Samsung as it stands commands more than 90% of the world’s OLED phone screen production capacity. The company 
has announced plans to beef up production of its flexible OLED displays—which have been adopted by Apple for the 
tenth anniversary iPhone X—to a level of 330,000, with a market share estimated to exceed 50% over the next three 
years. LG Display, following a flagging attack on the large-sized OLED TV panel market, has also started to shift its focus 
towards small-sized OLED displays, with its Gumi-based E5 flexible OLED line already put into mass production in 3Q17, 
and its E6 production line scheduled for 2018, eying a production capacity of at least 75,000 units for small- and medium-
sized flexible OLED displays. 
BOE, with a total capacity of 96,000 from its two flexible OLED lines, trails only behind Samsung, while its rival Hon 
Hai/Foxconn is setting sights on a flexible OLED display production capacity of no lower than 50,000.
