证券研究报告
有望实现长期稳定的盈利增长；首次覆盖评为买入 (摘要) 
建议理由 
圣邦股份是中国第二大模拟半导体设计公司，在物联网、电动车和AMOLED 领域
拥有2,000 多家客户和1,000 种产品。我们预计圣邦将通过下列方式继续从海外同
业手中赢得市场份额并实现盈利：1) 满足庞大的本地客户群的需求；2) 依靠其在
国内市场上的差异化技术优势；3) 利用模拟半导体市场较为分散的特征（与规模
更大的同业矽力杰相似）。我们认为该股股价尚未反映公司的长期增长潜力。我们
首次覆盖圣邦股份给予买入评级，12 个月目标价格人民币116 元，上涨空间
25%。 
 
推动因素 
圣邦自2015 年以来大力投资于研发，2018 年50 种新产品将实现上量（用于物联
网的低功率电源管理芯片(PMIC)、电池PMIC 和AMOLED 驱动器）。我们预计
2017-21 年圣邦的收入年均复合增速将达到23%的强劲水平（2017 年预计同比增
速为17%），但仍低于矽力杰2013-15 年间规模与之相似时50%的增速，归因于
圣邦的研发投入密度较低以及产品结构有所不同。 
估值 
我们使用长期贴现市盈率以体现圣邦的长期增长潜力，由此得出12 个月目标价格
人民币116 元：40 倍的2018 年预期市盈率同业均值乘以2021 年预期每股盈利并
贴现回2018 年，隐含的2018 年预期市盈率为57 倍。虽然目前圣邦股价隐含的
2018 年预期市盈率为46 倍（基于我们的预测），而A 股半导体同业均值为40
倍，我们认为这一溢价与美国模拟半导体股票相对于半导体设计股票30%以上的
溢价基本一致。尽管圣邦当前估值较高，我们认为隐含的2018 年预期市盈率目标
57 倍有望实现，因为公司将：1) 利用其多元化产品组合；2) 实现产品结构改善；
并且(3) 在分散的模拟半导体市场上攫取市场份额。 
主要风险 
1) 中国模拟半导体企业之间的竞争加剧。2) 新产品推出延迟。3) 由于高度依赖台
积电而面临供应链风险。

SG Micro: No. 2 player in China’s analog device market  
Founded in 2007 and based in Beijing, SG Micro specializes in analog IC design and sales. It has 
two main product lines: PMIC (power management IC) and signal chain, which are widely used in 
cell phones, TVs, digital cameras, computers, consumer electronics, automobile electronics, 
industrial automation, and IoT devices (e.g., sharing bikes). SG Micro has a diversified portfolio of 
over 1,000 products and over 2,000 customers. It competes mostly with global peers such as TI, 
ADI, and Maxim.  
We are positive on SG Micro’s growth outlook for the following four reasons. 
 
Compared with its overseas peers, SG Micro has many local customers to provide 
superior service and time-to-market design, enjoys abundant supply of junior engineers, 
and has a high valuation level in the A-share market to facilitate its potential acquisition 
of small local companies and attract talent.  
 
Analog is a fragmented market where smaller players can take share and remain 
profitable. Richtek and Silergy are such examples. For more on China’s semiconductor 
design industry, see our report: Design and Conquer – in both new and traditional 
markets; Buy SG Micro, published on November 13, 2017. While SG Micro has less than 
1% market share in PMIC and signal chains, it delivered a net income CAGR of 14.5% 
over 2012-2016. We expect SG Micro to continue the growth with a CAGR of 27% in 
2017E-2021E driven by increased production and margin improvement from new 
products launches. 
 
SG Micro offers a relatively more differentiated technology and product portfolio than its 
Chinese peers. It leads in low power, high current, and high precision analog design. 
While most Chinese companies focus on PMIC, SG Micro has a niche position in signal 
chain products. The revenue breakup between PMIC and signal chain products is 57% 
and 43% as of 1H17, respectively. 
 
The Chairman and CEO of SG Micro, Dr. Zhang Shilong, has a track record in analog 
R&D over the past 20 years. He has previously worked as an engineer in top analog 
firms in the US, including Burr-Brown and TI, and was also recruited by the Chinese 
government through “the Thousand Talents Plan” (the Recruitment Program of Global 
Experts by China). To maintain its technology advantage over peers, we believe a low 
attrition rate of employees, especially senior engineers is critical in an analog 
semiconductor industry. SG Micro has had a very low employee turnover rate of below 
5% since inception in 2007, which we believe is partially attributed to competitive salary 
package and the relatively generous employee stock compensation. As of 3Q17, 7% of 
total shares are owned by employees while 46% is owned by Dr. Zhang and 
shareholders acting in concert.  We think having an experienced leader at the head of an 
experienced and stable team should stand SG Micro in good stead to gain analog 
market share from global competitors like TI, ADI and Maxim. 
Our 12-month target price of Rmb 116 is based on long-term discounted P/E of 40X, derived from 
2018 average Wind consensus P/E of A-share semiconductor design peers, applied on 2021E 
EPS and discounted back to 2018E using a peer average COE of 11.3%, implying a 2018E P/E 
of 57X. SG Micro is trading at a 46.5X 2018E GHe P/E, vs. China A-share peer (Wind consensus) 
average of 40X. We note that analog stocks often enjoy a valuation premium over other 
semiconductor companies due to analog’s diversified product and customer bases. For example, 
in the US, analog stocks have over 30% higher 2018E P/E and 600bps lower growth rate than 
other semiconductor design (Exhibit 3). Hence, SG Micro’s valuation premium over other A-share 
semiconductor stocks is justified in our view. We believe the stock will trade further up to our 
implied target multiple of 57X 2018E P/E given its diversified portfolio, improving product mix with 
higher margins and  continually increasing market share while retaining its technological 
advantage.

Key risks to our view include: 
1) Intensified competition among Chinese analog companies: We are aware of several 
sizable Chinese analog names, and while the market is fragmented, we view the potential 
increase in competition as a key risk.  
2) Delay in new products launch: SG Micro’s new products should have higher gross margin, if 
the launch delays or sell-through is below our expectations, the margin recovery road (to global 
peers’ average level) will be challenging.  
3) Supply chain management risk: TSMC provides 99% of wafer supply to SG Micro, and SG 
Micro’s new products with better cost structure will also rely on TSMC’s advanced technology. 
Although SG Micro is actively looking to cooperate with other suppliers, any capacity issue from 
TSMC could potentially cause problems for the company’s normal operation given the high 
reliance on wafer supply from TSMC.

Product overview: A diversified portfolio of PMIC and signal chains 
SG Micro has two main product lines, namely PMIC and signal chains, representing 60% and 
40% of its total revenues in 1H17E, respectively. The company has a diversified portfolio of over 
1,000 products. According to Gartner, PMIC had US$10.2bn TAM in 2016, similar to signal chain 
TAM of US$10.3bn, but signal chain products tend to have a longer life cycle and more stable 
margins. Often, a young analog company can ramp up a PMIC business faster than a signal 
chain business. SG Micro has accumulated technology and new designs in its PMIC business in 
the past two years and expects its PMIC business growth to surpass that of signal chain in 2017-
2019. The company expects its revenue mix of PMIC to increase to 70% in 2018 from 60% in 
1H17.

Diversified customer base and limited direct competition in China 
SG Micro sells most of its products through distributors to over 2,000 diversified end-customers; it 
sells directly only to ZTE. SG Micro’s distributor partners are well known in China and include 
Beigaozhi, Colorbeam, Linpo, Kei Kong, etc. The top-five distributors contribute around 62% of 
revenue. While SG Micro works directly with end-customers for design-in, its distributors fill the 
orders and handle logistics. 
The smartphone sector represents 40% of SG Micro’s total revenues today, but it is aiming for the 
smartphones, consumer, and auto/industrials/medicals sectors to represent around 30% each in 
the near future.  
SG Micro sources 99% of its wafers from TSMC, the world’s leading foundry, and relies on JCET, 
TongFu, and Unisem for packaging and testing.  
Exhibit 5: SG Micro has a wide range of distributors and customers 
SG Micro’s supply chain, distributors, and end-customers  
 
Source:  Company data, Gao Hua Securities Research. 
In analog, SG Micro competes mostly with overseas companies (such as TI, ADI, and Maxim); it 
competes with Silergy in the standard DC/DC converter market. Its gross margins are lower than 
most of its peers due to its new entrant status in the China market and lack of cost advantages. 
Chinese analog companies, Silergy, SG Micro, and FineMade are fabless design companies that 
purchase wafers as well as packaging and testing services, which are more expensive than 
internal productions at most overseas IDM (integrated design manufacturing) peers. Despite its 
high-margin signal chain products, SG Micro has lower margins than Silergy because Silergy has 
a proprietary wafer manufacturing process that can reduce PMIC wafer cost by 20% relative to 
TSMC’s generic process. However, SG Micro has said that TSMC has recently launched an 
180nm analog process that offers a similar cost structure as Silergy’s in-house process; we 
expect the cost structure to improve over the next two years, assuming Silergy’s cost structure 
remains in a steady state. In addition, SG Micro indicated that its new products tend to have 
higher margins so that, as its new product increases in the mix, its margin should improve 
gradually into 2018 and 2019. We expect around 100bps improvement in gross margins through 
2019E (vs 3Q17).

Earnings analysis: EPS CAGR of 27% in 2017E-2020E 
Revenue and profitability: Given its lower R&D intensity and improving product mix, we expect 
SG Micro’s revenue growth to accelerate to 26% in 2018 from 17% yoy in 2017E, and achieve a 
23% CAGR in 2017E-2021E, below Silergy’s organic growth CAGR of 50% when it was operating 
in a similar scale in 2013-2015. In 2013-15, Silergy benefited from the rapid growth of the SSD 
module business mainly driven by the company’s focus on PMIC that has a large TAM and a 
quick ramp-up with the right end-application. Looking forward, SG Micro plans to drive its growth 
through PMIC design-in for IoT, innovative devices (e.g., sharing bikes and Segway) and battery. 
SG Micro’s GPM increased to 45% in 1H17 due to a temporary reduction of low-margin 
smartphone business in the portfolio mix, but returned to 41% in 3Q17. The company expects its 
GPM to improve gradually as the mix of new products increases in the portfolio through 2018E-
2021E. We expect its GPM to improve by 2ppt to 43% in 2021E from 41% 2018E and help drive 
its EPS growth at 27% CAGR in 2017E-2020E.

Analog firm trading at a premium; expect further rerating  
We initiate SG Micro with a Buy rating and a 12-month target price of Rmb116. We value our A-
share China fabless companies using a long-term discounted P/E methodology in order to 
capture long-term growth. For SG Micro, we apply its A-share peers’ average 2018E P/E of 40X 
to 2021E EPS and discount it back to 2018 using a peer average cost of equity of 11.3%, as 
shown in Exhibit 12. Our target price of Rmb116 implies a 2018E P/E of 57X and 23% upside 
potential.  
SG Micro is trading at a GHe P/E of 46.5X 2018E, vs. China A-share peer (Wind consensus) 
average of 40X. We note that analog stocks often enjoy a valuation premium over other 
semiconductor companies due to analog’s diversified product and customer bases. For example, 
in the United States, analog stocks are trading at a 30% premium to the average 2018E P/E for 
other semiconductor design firms while posting a growth rate that is 600 bps lower (Exhibit 3). 
Hence, we believe SG Micro’s valuation premium over other A-share semiconductor stocks is 
justified.  
We believe our implied 2018E P/E of 57X should be achievable in the next 12 months based on 
the following reasons:  
1) 
We expect its new PMIC products with higher ASP to boost the earnings growth from 
2017 after significant investment in R&D since 2015; the company is shifting its business 
toward new applications with high margins, such as automobiles, medical equipment, 
industrial controls, and IoT from its current high exposure to smartphone (40%-45% of 
total revenues). 
2) 
As one of China’s leading analog brands, SG Micro benefits from the trend of adopting 
“Made in China” products by customers like Huawei and ZTE, which we believe will help 
it gain market share from global competitors (namely TI, ADI and Maxim).  
3) 
We believe the company can maintain its technology advantage given its strong design 
team. SG Micro continues to attract top engineers by offering competitive pay package 
and has a low turnover rate (below 5% since inception in 2007). A stable team is crucial 
for an analog company compared to a logic design house; analog design houses require 
more years of apprenticeship before a new analog professional can become productive.

Introducing M&A rank for SG Micro 
Across our global coverage, we examine stocks using an M&A framework, considering both 
qualitative factors and quantitative factors (which may vary across sectors and regions) to 
incorporate the potential that certain companies could be acquired. We then assign an M&A rank 
as a means of scoring companies under our rated coverage from 1 to 3, with 1 representing high 
(30%-50%) probability of the company becoming an acquisition target, 2 representing medium 
(15%-30%) probability and 3 representing low (0%-15%) probability. For companies ranked 1 or 2, 
in line with our standard departmental guidelines we incorporate an M&A component into our 
target price. M&A rank of 3 is considered immaterial and therefore does not factor into our price 
target, and may or may not be discussed in research. 
In the Asia technology sector, we examine companies’ potential as  M&A targets based on the 
assessment of (1) impediments, such as shareholder structure/preferences, industry 
regulations/government policies, and takeover defense that could prevent a transaction from 
occurring, (2) qualitative factors, including: (i) attractiveness of technology, (ii) attractiveness of 
customers, (iii) cost synergy potential, (iv) other/intangible value; and (3) quantitative factors, 
including (i) market cap, and (ii) valuations. For details of our Asia technology M&A framework, 
see our report Pan Asia: Technology: Navigating the noise (6): Introduce M&A framework, update 
roadmap, dated July 4, 2016 for details.  
Shareholder structure and potential impediments 
We gauge whether there are any impediments to M&A, because if there are, a company is 
unlikely to become an M&A target even if it ranks 1 or 2 on qualitative/quantitative criteria. 
When assessing potential impediments we take into consideration:  
(1) Major shareholder preferences: We assume that shareholders with a 20% or greater stake 
have a significant influence, or a blocking stake, and take into account any potential actions by 
major shareholders in the event of a possible acquisition by a third party (hostile or otherwise), or 
if they have announced a preference to raise their stake in the company;  
(2) Regulatory and or government policy impediments; and  
(3) Possible takeover defense measures such as a poison pill. 
We assign a “No” qualification if one or more of the three considerations above are applicable and 
a “Yes” qualification if these considerations do not apply. 
Qualitative assessment criteria 
1) Technology: We look at whether or not a company has attractive technology capable of 
generating future value, as well as its technological capabilities and intellectual property rights. 
We assign a rank of “3” if its technology is unattractive or moderately attractive, “2” if it is 
attractive and “1” if it is very attractive. 
2) Customer base: We look at whether or not a company has an attractive customer base and 
product applications. We also take customer retention and end-market exposure (including sales 
channels) into account. We assign a rank of “3” if its customer base and product applications are 
unattractive or moderately attractive, “2” if they are attractive and “1” if they are very attractive. 
3) Cost synergies: We look at prospects for growth in business scale and scope for profit margin/ 
yield improvement via acquisition of an M&A target company. We assign a rank of “3” if there is 
low or some possibility of synergies, “2” if there is high possibility, and “1” if there is very high 
possibility.

4) Other: We look at whether or not a target company possesses attractive assets, including 
brand strength, outstanding management/employees, and intangible assets. We assign a rank of 
“3” if they are unattractive or moderately attractive, “2” if they are attractive, and “1” if they are 
very attractive. 
Quantitative assessment criteria 
1) Market cap: We rank the companies in four tiers based on the assumption that the smaller a 
company is, the easier it is to acquire. We also take regional qualities (such as differing market 
cap ranges between regions) into account. 
2) Valuation: We look at whether a target company’s three-year average P/E and EV/DACF are 
below the industry/peer average for the same period, as we believe companies trading at a 
discount to peers are more likely to be acquired.  
We assign a rank of “3” if the company appears overvalued relative to its respective industry peer 
group or largely in line with the peer average, “2” if its valuation is discounted, and “1” if its 
valuation is significantly discounted. We take into account differing valuation multiple ranges 
between the companies depending on their region and their peers. 
Within this framework, we assign an M&A rank of ‘3’ to SG Micro 
Our M&A rank of ‘3’ for SG Micro mainly reflects its protective shareholding structure (nearly 46% 
held by Chairman Mr. Zhang Shilong and other shareholders acting in concert). Moreover, 
as Mr. Zhang has laid out a very long-term vision/plan for the company, we believe he is likely to 
retain a controlling stake as long as possible. Also, we believe the company is more likely to be 
an acquirer rather than a target as the former would be a good way to quickly expand product 
lines to compete with global peers, and the company also indicated that it could consider 
acquisitions as a strategy.

Key risks to our investment thesis 
We identify the following downside risks: 
1) Intensified competition among Chinese analog companies: We are aware of several 
sizable Chinese analog names, and although the market is fragmented, we view the potential 
increase competition will be the key risk. For example, Silergy is the Chinese leader and focuses 
on DC/DC PMICs; On Bright focuses on AC/DC PMIC; 3PEAK strives to develop high-end signal-
chain products; FineMade makes PMICs for LED and battery applications, etc. Thus, if Silergy (or 
other companies that are currently focusing on PMICs) also start to produce signal chain products, 
we believe the competition may intensify. 
2) Delay in new products launch: SG Micro’s new products should have higher gross margin, if 
the launch delays or sell-through is below our expectations, the margin recovery (to global peers’ 
average level) road will be challenging.  
3) Supply chain management risk: TSMC provides 99% of wafer supply to SG Micro, and SG 
Micro’s new products with better cost structure will also rely on TSMC’s advanced technology. 
Although SG Micro is actively looking to cooperate with other suppliers, any capacity issue from 
TSMC could potentially cause problems for the company’s normal operation given the high 
reliance on wafer supply from TSMC. 
4) Greater than expected employee turnover: High-level engineers and engineer managers 
are very sought after in China. Any slowdown in the growth of the company and decline in 
employee stock compensation can potentially negatively impact the employee turnover especially 
among senior engineers.

投资摘要 
投资摘要部分通过将一只股票的主要指标与其行业和市场相比较来评价该股的投资环境。所描述的四个主要指标包括增长、回报、估值倍数和波动性。增长、
回报和估值倍数都是运用数种方法综合计算而成，以确定该股在地区研究行业内所处的百分位排名。  
每项指标的准确计算方式可能随着财务年度、行业和所属地区的不同而有所变化，但标准方法如下：  
增长是下一年预测与当前年度预测的综合比较，如每股盈利、EBITDA 和收入等。 回报是各项资本回报指标一年预测的加总，如CROCI、平均运用资本回报率
和净资产回报率。 估值倍数根据一年预期估值比率综合计算，如市盈率、股息收益率、EV/FCF、EV/EBITDA、EV/DACF、市净率。 波动性根据12 个月的历史
波动性计算并经股息调整。  
并购评分 
在我们的全球覆盖范围中，我们使用并购框架来分析股票，综合考虑定性和定量因素（各行业和地区可能会有所不同）以计入某些公司被收购的可能性。然后
我们按照从1 到3 对公司进行并购评分，其中1 分代表公司成为并购标的的概率较高(30%-50%)，2 分代表概率为中等(15%-30%)，3 分代表概率较低(0%-
15%)。对于评分为1 或2 的公司，我们按照研究部统一标准将并购因素体现在我们的目标价格当中。并购评分为3 被认为意义不大，因此不予体现在我们的目
标价格当中，分析师在研究报告中可以予以讨论或不予讨论。 
Quantum 
Quantum 是提供具体财务报表数据历史、预测和比率的高盛专有数据库，它可以用于对单一公司的深入分析，或在不同行业和市场的公司之间进行比较。  
GS SUSTAIN 
GS SUSTAIN 是一项侧重于通过发现优质行业领先企业而实现长期超额收益的全球投资策略。GS SUSTAIN 50 关注名单列出了我们认为凭借出色的资本回
报、具有可持续性的竞争优势和对ESG（环境、社会和企业治理）风险的有效管理而有望在长期内相对于全球同业表现出色的行业领军企业。候选企业主要基
于对企业在这三方面表现的综合量化分析筛选而出。
