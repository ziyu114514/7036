Summary
3Q25 Gross Margin Improved Quarter-on-Quarter, with Expense Optimization Supporting Profit Resilience. In the first three
quarters of 2025, Huali Group recorded revenue of RMB 18.68 billion, up 10.4% year-on-year, corresponding to 3Q25 revenue of
RMB 6.02 billion, down 0.3% year-on-year. Gross profit for 9M25 was RMB 4.10 billion, down 15.8% YoY, with a gross margin of
22.0%, a decrease of 5.8 ppts YoY; 3Q25 gross profit was RMB 1.34 billion, down 18.1% YoY, with a gross margin of 22.2%, down
4.8 ppts YoY but rebounding quarter-on-quarter. The operating expense ratios totaled 4.8% in both 9M25 and 3Q25, slightly up
QoQ mainly due to higher R&D spending. R&D expenses rose 34% YoY to RMB 318 million; administrative expenses fell 45% YoY
million, reflecting lower labor and travel costs. Financial expenses increased in 3Q25, mainly due to higher interest costs and
foreign exchange losses. Affected by high interest rates in VND and IDR, the company adopted a "borrow USD, deposit VND"
treasury strategy, which expanded short-term borrowings and raised interest costs. Meanwhile, VND depreciation against USD
and appreciation of TWD and RMB led to larger FX losses. Operating profit for 9M25 was RMB 3.21 billion, down 11.8% YoY, and
RMB 1.05 billion for 3Q25, down 11.6% YoY. The 3Q25 effective tax rate was 17.8%, down significantly from 25.1% in 2Q25. Net
profit attributable to shareholders for 9M25 was RMB 2.44 billion, down 14.3% YoY; for 3Q25 it was RMB 760 million, down 20.8%
YoY, with a net margin of 12.7%. Operating cash flow for 9M25 was RMB 3.76 billion, down 11.5% YoY, mainly due to higher tax
and payroll payments.
3Q25 ASP Increased, While Customer Inventory Destocking Weighed on Order Growth. In 3Q25, Huali Group recorded revenue
of RMB 6.02 billion (-0.3% YoY), with shipment volume of approximately 51 million pairs (-6.7% YoY). ASP in RMB exceeded
expectations, rising 6.9% YoY. The shipment decline mainly reflected: (1) continued inventory destocking by core customers—
especially in vulcanized footwear, where weak demand and inventory correction dragged overall order growth, though
improvement is expected next year as brands further optimize their supply chains; and (2) a slight drop in orders from core brands
due to lingering inventory pressure, partly offset by supply chain optimization and new product launches. To mitigate the impact
from weaker legacy brand orders, the company (1) actively expanded new brand partnerships to drive incremental growth and (2)
increased supply chain share with existing clients. Meanwhile, cooperation projects with major new brands are progressing
steadily, with new factories in Sichuan and Indonesia ramping up as planned, keeping shipments in line with expectations. Overall
customer diversification continued to improve, enhancing earnings resilience and reducing exposure to single-brand volatility.
Cost Optimization and New Factory Profitability Drive Margin Recovery; Restocking Cycle Expected in 2026. The 3Q25 gross
margin was 22.2%, down 4.8 ppts YoY but up 1.1 ppts QoQ, in line with expectations, supported by: (1) no new factory
have reached profitability, while the fourth remains in ramp-up and is expected to break even within two quarters; and (3) the
first Indonesian factory achieving profitability this year, reinforcing management's confidence to expand Indonesian capacity. Cost-
control measures continued, with total headcount falling by about 2,000 from end-June despite ongoing factory ramp-ups.
According to capacity plans, two new factories are scheduled for launch in 4Q25. Although 4Q25 margins will remain under
pressure, their smaller scale and continued cost discipline should limit downside. Management indicated 2024–2025 as the peak
of capacity expansion, after which capex intensity will gradually decline. The company noted that tariff-related headwinds and
weaker brand consumption have posed some pressure on the industry, but the overall quantitative impact remains limited.
Management expects the sector's inventory destocking cycle to extend through 2025, with a potential restocking phase beginning
in 2026.
