An electroceramics heavyweight with superior earnings performance
Chaozhou Three-Circle Group Co., Ltd. ("Three-Circle Group"), a bellwether in the ceramic industry, is now focused on
electroceramics with products spanning fiber optic ceramic ferrules and sleeves, fuel cell membranes, electroceramic
and listed in 2014.
Source: Three-Circle Group's website, Research Dept, CSCI
Three-Circle Group boasts to be the world's biggest producer and seller of ceramic-based optical communications
components, alumina ceramic substrates and ceramic matrices used in resistors and the only manufacturer in China
capable of mass production of electroceramic packaging, taking the largest domestic market share in compressor
connectors as well.

Ceramic chassis/MLCC
Ceramic chassis
Metal as a conductor will absorb or reflect radio waves and thus causes wireless interference. As a result, ceramic and
glass chassis, which are free of such an off-putting nature while bestowed with properties such as high rigidity and
excellent surface polish, are gaining in popularity among smartphone makers as the world ushers in the new era of
wireless charging and 5G.
Source: zol.com.cn, Research Dept, CSCI
Listed company in-depth
Global shipments of smartphones came to 1.517bn units in 2017 and look set to reach 1.744bn by 2021, according to
IDC's forecast. This could hint at a ceramic chassis market size of RMB31.4bn by 2021 on an assumed penetration rate
of 30% and a unit price of RMB60.
Source: IDC, Research Dept, CSCI
Source: xincailiao.com, Research Dept, CSCI
China as it stands still relies heavily on the import of ceramic powder. But Three-Circle Group is an exception. The
company possesses proprietary technologies throughout the zirconia powder production process with effective cost and
yield control, able to produce 1mn pieces of high performance ceramic chassis per month, and is thus best placed to
benefit from the would-be introduction of ceramic housing by China's incumbent "big three" smartphone makers,
Huawei, Oppo and Vivo.
MLCC
A dearth of MLCC supply not least from Japanese manufacturers like Murata, the biggest MLCC producer in the world,
is estimated to last for at least a year and a half. This, compounded by a surge in the prices of palladium, a key ingredient
of MLCC, together with increasing demands emanating from such burgeoning areas as EV and auto electronics and the
growing use of passive components for smartphones, would spur and sustain a MLCC price upturn, in our view, providing
tailwinds for Three-Circle Group, whose MLCC division earned around RMB100mn in 2016, accounting for 4% of its full-
year revenue.

Ceramic-based optical communications components
We are living in a world where 5G commercialization is approaching fast, FTTH implementations are gathering pace, and
the DC construction boom is in full charge. These all fire up demand for fiber optic ceramic ferrules, an indispensable
part of fiber optic connectors.
China's fiber optic ceramic ferrules market is the largest in the world, with an output of 2.25bn in 2015 and set to grow
at a CAGR of 15% to reach 3.434bn by 2018.
Source: chyxx.com, Research Dept, CSCI

Three-Circle Group, already a recognized global leader in fiber optic ceramic ferrules, boasts a client roster of famous
international fiber optic connector vendors such as TE Connectivity, Amphenol Industrial, HUBER+SUHNER and Radiall.
With an eye-catching gross margin of above 50%, the company's fiber optic ceramic ferrules and sleeves business
claimed a market share of more than 80% by year-end 2017, up from the previous 50%, thanks mainly to a practice of
price-cutting since the beginning of 2016 on the back of its self-sufficiency in ceramic powder and economies of scale.
Looking ahead, as 5G deployment is imminent which will dial up demand for optical communications components, the
company may as well make a U-turn in its pricing strategy, in our view.
Source: Three-Circle Group's website, Research Dept, CSCI
Electroceramic packaging
Three-Circle Group is the only manufacturer in China capable of volume production of ceramic packaging for surface-
mount devices (SMDs), a core component of communications and digital products, counting Partron, NDK and Micro
Crystal as its customers.
There are only four companies in the world able to mass produce electroceramic packaging, namely, Kyocera, NSSED,
NTK and Three-Circle Group, with Kyocera having the largest market share and the broadest offering of product
categories.
Three-Circle Group's semiconductor devices unit (including electroceramic packaging) recorded a 2016 operating
revenue of RMB600mn, a far cry compared to about RMB14.1bn for that of Kyocera. Hence, there is plenty of scope for
Three-Circle Group to keep up with its arch rival.

R&D-driven innovator
The global advanced ceramics market reached a size of USD61bn in 2016, significantly up from USD34bn in 2006, and is
expected to further expand to USD68bn by 2018, according to Roland Berger.
With such an upbeat picture in mind, Three-Circle Group is proactively opening up new avenues of growth in areas such
as 5G, biomedicine and environmental protection to achieve sustainable development.
Like its leading global peers, Three-Circle Group invests more than 3% of its annual operating revenue into R&D, bringing
out new products every two or three years to bolster earnings growth. The company has established a research institute
alongside a more than 600-strong R&D team, covering a wide-ranging variety of cutting-edge technologies and futuristic
subjects.
